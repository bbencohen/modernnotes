Guadalcanal August 7 1942

“The Island of Death”

New Japanese Strategy —> New strategy to occupy territory/islands because their aircraft carriers are done. Their new aim/strategy is to occupy islands to use as air fields which can act as fixed-location aircraft carriers.

  • Turning point: First major offensive by Allied forces against the Empire of Japan
    • Counter Argument:
      • Doolittle raids
      • Midway was kind of a US offensive because even though Japan did the first attack the US went ahead and was stationed and ready to launch into combat at any moment
  • August 7, 1942: Allied forces (mostly American) landed on the islands of Guadalcanal, Tulagi, and Florida
  • Japanese were attempting to build an airfield on the island to cut off communications between USA and Australia
  • By June 1942 there were an estimated 3000 Japanese soldiers on the island of Guadalcanal.
  • Turning point: Allied control thought that they could do better to protect Australia from a Japanese invasion
  • Protect the Allied built=up in Australia acting as a springboard for a major assault on the Pacific fortress of Japan
  • Turning point: Japanese were failing in the naval department, Americans had reacjed / surpassed Japanese Naval supremacy. The Japanese attempted to build an airfield on Guadalcanal
  • The industrial capacity of USA has tripled by this point since 1939, they have more resources and weapons that are being absolutely flooded into the South Pacific

Twin Drives

  • Chief of Naval Operations Admiral Ernest King favoured a drive across the cenrtal Pacific moving toward Japan over the coral atolls scattered across the Pacific
    • Take advantage of the ability to leap across vast distances
  • MacArthur favoured an advance across the South Pacific via New Guinea and the philippines
    • Meet obligations to Filipinos
    • Maintain pressure against the retreating Japanese
    • Protect against a renewed threat against Australia

Strategies and Tactics

Japanese

  • Aimed to take the defensive position (Turning point, this is the first time they have done this in the war)
  • Troops consisted of conscripted Koreans (the Koreans were occupied by the Japanese and they hated being occupied by Japan. Turning point, Japan is stretched out too thin and they are no longer fighting their own battles. Also the Koreans don’t want to fight for Japan)
  • After Midway, the importance of New Caledonia and Fiji was greater than ever, but its available force had shrunk
  • Had to move away from large-scale carrier operations (Turning point)
  • Would aim to target naval vessels with land based aircraft (turning point ^^)
  • Airfields built on islands enabled aircraft to block critical passages and make enemy offensives dangerous

American strategy

  • Neutralise Japan’s land-based aircraft and take the islands as bases for their own aircraft
  • The US Navy supported troops with firepower as they landed on islands, blocking forces for hundreds of miles around
  • Island-hopping strategy movement and establishment of mini military bases on islands

US & AUS

  • Three carriers gave air support (the Saratoga, the Wasp, and the Enterprise guarded by the battleship USS North Carolina. Eight cruisers from America and Australia guarded the actual landing craft as they approached the beaches of Guadalcanal) (Turning point)
  • Tactical surprise
  • US waited until airbase almost complete then appropriated it
  • The attack on Guadalcanal is a tactical surprise for Japan, they surprise Japan while they are trying to establish an air base
  • The US were literally just sitting there waiting until it was pretty much done so they could wipe out the soldiers and use the airbase themselves

Island Hopping - Allied Strategy

  • Skipping over heavily fortified islands in order to seize lightly defended locations that could support the next advance
  • Isolated strongholds while the defenders were left to weaken from starvation and disease. Ise thje vast Pacific distances as an ally while creeping in on Japan

The Navajo Code Talkers

  • While Americans were able to break the Japanese code, Americans used the Navajo code without fear of Japan breaking it.
  • Around 400 navajo were recruited
  • Only about 25 non-Navajo people in the entire world could speak the language
  • Made up words for terms that didn’t exist in their language (names of birds meant “airplanes” and the word “egg” actually meant “bomb”)

Turning point?

  • America’s most powerful amphibious force (change in tactics) ever assembled solifidying island hopping strategy
  • Americans expanded their perimeter (offensive shift) to continuously respply their positions with weapons, ammunition, and supplies, thereby constantly sustaining a degree of combat strength which was superior to the Japanese
  • Set the tone for a new type of batttle - land/sea air battle of attratition
  • Shattered the myth of Japanese invincibility, particularly in the Jungle
    • In Malaya they literally won a battle on bicycles
  • Ended Japanese strategic operations
  • U.S. no longer had to defend the line of supply to Australia, and American troops surged to Australian bases
  • That Marine, Navy, and Army aviators took out elite Japanese air crews, allowing America to achieve air superiority more easily in future battles
  • US industrial supremacy becomes apparent (nothing to do with Guadalcanal, but a turning point early in the war because it makes it clear that Japan will not match US industrial capacity)
  • FIRST GAPS IN THE DEFENSIVE PERIMETER OF THE CO-PROSPERITY SPHERE WERE APPEARING
“The Japanese war machine truly grinded to a halt” - O’Neill

Impacts

  • 60k US Marines and soldiers killed about 20k of the 31k Japanese troops on the island

  • The US had a 10% casualty rate and only 24/3600 Japanse soldiers surrendered - the Japanese also lost highly trained pilots that were irreplacable
