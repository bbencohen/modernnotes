Evaluate the implications of the fall of Singapore, Burma, Java, and the Philippines to the Allies and the Japanese

The successful early Japanese invasion and occupation of Singapore, Burma, Java, the Philippines, and other surrounding territories from 1941-1942 carried many implications for the Allied powers and the Japanese. By Japan demonstrating their ability to occupy such a vast extent of territory across the entire Pacific ocean and into the Indian ocean through Burma and India, the United Kingdom and the United States became much more concerned with Japanese expansion. Prior to the massive Japanese expansion in the Pacific, the Allies relied on Singapore as the “impenetrable fortress” that would act as a line of defence, especially for Australia.
