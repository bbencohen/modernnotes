Battle of Tarawa (November 1943)

  • Japan vs. USA at the Tarawa Atoll in the Gilbert Islands (Kiribati)
  • Was a part of Operation Galvanic (USA invasion of the Gilbert Islands)
  • Approx. 6400 Japanese/Korean and American soldiers died

American Strategic Decisions

  • America planned to take the Mariana islands from Japan (Japan was given the Mariana Islands from Germany after WWI) to use as an air base to support operations across the mid-Pacific
    • The Marianas were extremely heavily defended though, so the next best option was the Mashall Islands
      • The Marshall Islands were cut off from USA supply and communication lines at that point though because of a Japanese garrisson on Betio (small island next to Tarawa)
        • Battle of Tarawa would allow them to destroy the garrison and open supply lines between USA and Marshall Islands
  • Following the completion of the Guadalcanal campaign, the 2nd Marine Division had been withdrawn to New Zealand for rest.
    • Losses were replaced and soldiers were given time to recover from illnesses (especially malaria) that they had gotten from fighting in the Solomons
  • 20 July 1943: Nimitz begins to prepare plans for an offensive in the Gilbert Islands

Japanese Preparations

  • Betio is the largest island in the Tarawa Atoll, about 3900km southwest of Pearl Harbour
    • It had a pier jutting out from the north side that cargo ships could unload on while anchored
  • USA sent a diversionary raid to Makin Island (North of Tarawa) in 1942 which made Japan aware of the vulnerability of the Gilbert Islands
    • Reinforcements were sent:
      • 6th Yokosuka Special Naval Landing Force began reinforcing the island in Feb 1943
        • Became a garrisson force
      • Extreme amounts of pillboxes and firing pits were constructed
      • Command post constructed in the centre of the island
      • Several shelters to protect Japanese fighters from air raids

Battle

  • The USA force sent to the Gilbert Islands was the largest one yet for any operation in the Pacific
    • 17 aircraft carriers
    • 12 battleships
    • 8 heavy cruisers
    • 4 light cruisers
    • 66 destroyers
    • 36 transport ships
    • Total of about 35,000 troops
  • Resulted in an American victory

Gunichi Mikawa was a vice-admiral in the Imperial Japanese Navy, from Hiroshima.

In World War 2:

  • Was in command of the Battleship Division 3 during the Attack on Pearl Harbour
  • From July 1942 - April 1943 he commanded the IJN 8th Fleet, mostly stationed around New Britain (New Guinea)
    • During this time, he led Japanese Naval forces in the battle of Guadalcanal

Important bit:

  • 13 November 1942: Mikawa led a cruiser force that bombed an American airbase on Guadalcanal, and lost one heavy cruiser in the process
  • He then attempted to land Japanese reinforcements to the base Lae (PNG) but it turned into the Battle of the Bismarck Sea (absolutely disastrous for Japan)

Battle of the Java Sea

  • Extremely devastating battle for the Allies
    • Dutch Rear-Admiral of ABDACOM Karel Doorman was killed
    • More smaller battles around Java afterwards - all led to Allied defeats
  • These defeats ultimately led to the successful Japanese occupation of the Dutch East Indies
  • Happened about a week after the bombing of Darwin - the bombing left Darwin useless as a supply and naval base for supporting operations there
