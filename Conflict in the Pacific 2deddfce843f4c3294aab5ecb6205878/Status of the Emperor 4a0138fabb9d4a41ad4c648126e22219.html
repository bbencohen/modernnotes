Japanese Occupation Further Detail

  • Enormous problems of starvation, disease and housing as winter approached
    • MacArthur ordered 3.5 million tonnes of food
      • Within 6 months, food supplies stored around the Pacific and South East Asia stored for potential American invasion arrived
  • Under Potsdam Declaration:
    • The post-war Government was to come from the will of the people
    • MacArthur wanted to hold elections in 1946 and therefore needed a new constitution before then
      • The Japanese politicians wanted to ensure that (while the militarists would be removed) Japan would be governed conservatively
      • Japanese politicians moved too slowly --> America wrote a constitution for Japan in-house and then had it translated
        • Japan was essentially forced to accept it
        • That is the current Japanese constitution and it hasn’t been changed since
        • "Many scholars have noted the irony of the Occupation installing democratic political institutions in Japan through transparently authoritarian means"

          - Workbook Page 146

  • Occupation:
    • At its height, included over 500,000 Americans
    • Occupation was premised on the notion that the thorough demilitarization and democratization of Japan would make the world safe from future Japanese aggression
    • Drafting a new constitution was considered essential to recasting Japan as a peaceful member of the international community

Changes in Civic Values

  • US government used its control of newspapers and magazines to explain and popularise democracy
  • American democracy as a model to be copied
  • Education system and the laws regulating families be revised. "Moral training" in schools was abolished, and instruction in democratic ideas was begun
  • Control of education and censorship of textbooks were taken from the central government and given to local administrations
  • The laws giving the head of the household complete control of every family member (for example, he could withhold his consent when his children wished to be married) were changed to make each family member more equal and thereby more democratic
