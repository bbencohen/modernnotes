      Strategic Reasons

      • Pre-occupation in European conflict
        • If Japan could quickly conquer the British and Dutch colonies in South East Asia and gain complete control of the oil, rubber, and other raw materials needed, then it could defend its interests in China and Indochina against Europeans who were now busy fighting a major war in Europe against Germany/Italy
      • Destruction of the US Pacific Fleet
        • The only force that could stop Japan was the US Pacific fleet
        • The military throught that if it could suddenly destroy the US fleet, America would simply give up and allow Japan to consolidate its grasp on East Asia
      • American Appeasement
        • Japan was not militarily or economically powerful enough to fight a long war against the USA and the Japanese military knew this. USA had previously appeased Japan and they expected a similar position to be taken
      • ‘ABCD’ encirclement
        • By 1941, the Japanese were beginning to complain of an “ABCD” encirclement - their nation was being encircled by America (Hawaii, Guam, Philippines), British (Hong Kong), China, and the Dutch (East Indies).

Summary of Strategic reasons for Pearl Harbour:

  • Japan needed to expand into South East Asia to secure resources
    • Bombing the harbour would put America out of the war for 2 years, allowing Japan to expand and forcing USA to negotiate
    • Knocking america out would reduce the amount of enemies that Japan, Germany, and Italy would have to fight against to gain power in the Pacific and Europe.
    • This would also mean Americans would not be able to assist Britain if japan decided to expand to British places of interest
  • America wouldn’t be able to supply arms to Britain and France
  • Germany could continue and even extend its dominance in Europe
  • The USSR remained preoccupied with German invasion
  • Japanese troops would be able to overwhelm forces in Asia and secure their supply lines quickly enough
  • The USA would take some time to recover and if Japanese gains were dramatic enough they might prove willing to negotiate peace with a newly established status quo
  • Yamamoto saw that Japan had no chance of surviving against USA in a long war
  • Destroying air craft carriers
  • Attacking submarine bases and oil resources
  • Destroying majority of Pacific fleet
  • Destroying base to extent of relocation to West coast
  • PH would delay the USA long enough to allow Japanese to set up a defensive perimeter of naval and air bases to protect its vast seized territories
  • Hoped to reaffirm racial pride and inspire Asian superiority in the South-East

        • 50 B5N Type 97 Bombers armed with 800kg bombs
        • 40 B5N Type 97 Bombers armed with Type 91 Torpedos
          • Significantly modified to take out the ships in Pearl Harbour due to the shallow water of the area
        • 183 aircraft launched at 6am
          • Separated into groups to attack specific targets
        • Sky was completely clear of American fighters
          • Japan achieved complete surprise
        • Their main target - Aircraft carriers - weren't in port
          • Went "rogue" - diverted from their original plans and attacked the Battleships
        • The Americans had activated their anti-aircraft guns after being warned by the bombing attacks on the main base
        • Japan's attack on Battleship Row had a 46% hit rate
        • High-Air bombers had a 60% dud rate
        • USS Arizona was hit twice, and had a magazine explosion - destroying it
        • Nevada wasn't badly damaged - moved out to launch a counterattack
      • Second Wave
        • 167 planes launched to finish off the attack
          • 70-90% low overcast, coupled with saturated anti-air fire meant their accuracy & life expectancy were significantly reduce
          • Pilots had a bad target selection, Priority targets:
            • Priority 1 Targets: Aircraft Carriers (none present)
            • Priority 2 Targets: Cruisers (8 present)
            • Priority 3 Targets: Battleships
            • Destroyers, Sea Tenders, & Auxiliary Ships were not on the target list
