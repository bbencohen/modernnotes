To what extent did American/British policies push Japan into war?

The fact that the USA were very isolationist (didn’t actively try to stop them) and the British were ignorant as the Japanese capability of initiating a full scale invasion, allowed for the Japanese aggressive attitude towards the taking of land to be encouraged/appeased.

The fourth Neutrality Act of 1937 reaffirmed the mandatory ban on arms and loans to countries at war and made American travel on belligerent vessels illegal. When Japan began its further advance into China in 1937, American government and public sympathies were firmly with the Chinese. However, when Japanese pilots sank the US gunboat Panay (two killed, 30 wounded) during the assault on Nanking on 12 December, the sinking produced a cry for withdrawal, not for war. In 1938, a poll showed that a majority of Americans favoured the USA abandoning China altogether. When Japan tendered an official apology for the Panay incident paying $2 million in reparations, the crisis ended. As the diplomat William Bullitt told President Roosevelt, "We have large emotional interests in China, small economic interests, and no vital interests". USA has a non interventionist policy on China, which appeased Japan and allowed them to continue their aggressive expansion into the Pacific and China.

British Policies

Up to 1941 very ignorant.

They had three major concerns with Japan

  1. Any Japanese expansion to the south of the Pacific would threaten British colonial territory
  1. Japan’s emergence as a naval power threatened Britain’s traditional dominance of the sea and ability to protect its trade routes
  1. Since the 1920s Britain had been a supporter of Chiang Kai-Shek and an independent China.

THE REASONS FOR BRITISH POLICIES:

  1. Weak military position, forced to concentrate on threat in Europe
  1. Japanese imperialism was a potential threat to British interests in the Far East, and even to India
  1. Appeasement would reduce the amount of money that Britain would have to spend on improving defence to combat Japan

Judgements by the British and Americans were warped by an strong underlying sense of racism. “It was felt that Japan lacked the skilled workers to produce modern armaments,” “The Japanese have peculiarly slow brains”

In assessing the potential menace of Japan, the face should not be lost sight of that her economy had been severely taxed by the strain of two years hostilities in China. It is estimated ... that the output of war material in Japan has reached full capacity under the limitations of the supply of skilled labour and the means to pay for greater supplies of imported raw materials ... it had to be remembered that 50% of Japan’s iron ore came from Malaya and most of its manganese and a third of its cotton from India; in other words Britain did have the capacity to hurt Japan

- A. Best, in Intelligence and National Security, Vol. 11, No. 3, July 1996, p. 420.

Why, according to Source Best, were the British confident about their ability to face a challenge from Japan?

The British were confident about their ability to face a challenge from Japan during World War 2, largely due to the reasons acknowledged by A. Best in Intelligence and National Security. They say that “Britain did have the capacity to hurt Japan” due to 50% of Japan’s iron ore coming from Malaya, and substantial amounts of its manganese and cotton from India (both of these were British-owned at the time)
