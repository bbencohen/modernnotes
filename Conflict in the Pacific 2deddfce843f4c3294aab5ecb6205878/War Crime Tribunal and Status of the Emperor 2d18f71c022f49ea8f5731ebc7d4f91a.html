Status of the Emperor in the Trials

  • The first time the Japanese people heard his voice
  • Claimed that Japanese surrender was to stop the American cruelty and slaughter
  • Debate outside Japan as to whether to trial

Aims:

  • Hirohito had known since 1942 that war crimes trials were an Allied aim --> Moscow Declaration 1943
  • First batch of arrests September 11th, 1945 --> Top government figures
  • Higashikuni proposed internal war crimes tribunal to seize initiative from Allies
  • MacArthur wanted to protect the emperor
    • Protection of the emperor was fundamental
      • Documents were withheld from court to remove his impact from the war
    • Knew the emperor was essential to winning Japanese favour (post-war plans)
  • Prosecution and defence relationship cultivated positive relationship
    • Trials were used as propaganda to demonstrate success of US strategic bombing campaigns
    • Tojo was used as a scapegoat and was pressured to testify to save the emperor
    • Spread ' controlled' democracy - Japan would play an integral role in upcoming war with the Soviets
    • Place all responsibility on the army
    • Preserve and defend the state
    • Nuremberg was the legal model and a psychological pressure
    • Charges for "crimes against peace" differed amongst nations and depended on their own interests rather than morality

Consequences of the War Crimes Tribunal

  • International Military Tribunal from the Far East, "Tokyo Trial"
  • Potsdam --> Stern justice to war criminals
  • Generals executed in Philippines prior to tribunal in January of 1946
  • Severely shaped by US economic and political dominance as well as MacArthur's excessive power
  • The court was effectively a US Supreme Court conducted on the basis of Truman's executive powers
  • Allied scheming so Emperor wasn't testified against / implicated
  • Defence against communism was commonly raised throughout the trial
  • Victors Justice
    • Allied atrocities overlooked
    • US influence / Western imperialism 'reinstated'
